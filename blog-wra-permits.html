Why WRA Permits Matter (And What Happens If You Skip Them)

The Water Resources Authority (WRA) regulates all water abstraction in Kenya under the Water Act 2016. Whether you're drilling a borehole, abstracting from a river, or pumping from a spring, you MUST obtain a Water Permit before use.

‚ö†Ô∏è Consequences of Operating Without a Permit

  • Heavy Fines: Up to KSH 2,000,000 or 10 years imprisonment (or both!)
  • Borehole Closure: WRA can order immediate cessation of water abstraction
  • Equipment Seizure: Pumps and infrastructure can be confiscated
  • Legal Liability: Criminal charges under the Water Act 2016
  • No Resale Value: Properties with unpermitted boreholes lose market value
  • Bank Loan Rejection: Many banks refuse to finance properties with illegal water sources

‚úÖ Benefits of Proper WRA Compliance

  • Legal Protection: Operate your borehole without fear of penalties
  • Property Value: Permitted boreholes add 15-25% to property value
  • Dispute Resolution: WRA mediates conflicts with neighbors over water rights
  • Access to Support: Eligible for government water conservation programs
  • Bankable Asset: Lenders accept permitted boreholes as collateral
  • Peace of Mind: Sleep well knowing you're 100% compliant

Complete Step-by-Step WRA Permit Process (2024)

1

Initial Application Submission

Your journey begins with submitting a formal application to the Water Resources Authority.

Shows borehole location relative to property boundaries, buildings, and neighboring properties.

2

WRA Site Inspection

Within 2-3 weeks of application submission, WRA schedules a site inspection to verify:

  • Borehole location matches application details
  • Drilling was done professionally with proper casing
  • No environmental hazards (e.g., proximity to septic tanks, pollution sources)
  • Neighboring water sources won't be adversely affected
  • Water use aligns with stated purpose (domestic, commercial, irrigation, etc.)

üìã What Inspectors Check

  • Borehole Construction: Casing integrity, wellhead seal, sanitary protection
  • Pump Installation: Proper submersible pump setup with pressure gauge
  • Water Storage: Tank capacity, cleanliness, overflow management
  • Drainage: No stagnant water around wellhead (contamination risk)
  • Distance Compliance: Minimum 15m from septic tanks, 30m from pit latrines
  • Metering: Water meter installed for abstraction tracking (commercial use)

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Inspection Failures (And How to Avoid Them)

  • Issue: Missing wellhead seal ‚Üí Fix: Install concrete apron around casing (2m radius)
  • Issue: No water meter ‚Üí Fix: Install calibrated meter before inspection
  • Issue: Drainage problems ‚Üí Fix: Create proper drainage channels away from wellhead
  • Issue: Incomplete documentation ‚Üí Fix: Have drilling report, pump test data ready on-site
  • Issue: Proximity violations ‚Üí Fix: Relocate borehole if too close to contamination sources (expensive!)

3

Permit Issuance & Payment

Once your inspection passes, WRA issues a Water Permit Letter specifying:

  • Permitted Abstraction Volume: Maximum m¬≥/day you can extract
  • Permit Duration: Usually 5 years (renewable)
  • Water Use Restrictions: Domestic, irrigation, commercial, industrial
  • Annual Tariff: Water Resource User Fee (varies by volume)
  • Compliance Conditions: Metering, reporting, conservation requirements

Ongoing Compliance & Renewals

Your permit journey doesn't end at issuance! Annual compliance is mandatory to maintain valid permit status.

Annual Requirements:

üìã Yearly Compliance Checklist

‚úÖ

Due every 12 months. Failure to pay = permit suspension.

‚úÖ
Submit Abstraction Records

Monthly meter readings (commercial users). Quarterly reporting (domestic users).

‚úÖ

Required for commercial/public use. Recommended for domestic (peace of mind).

‚úÖ
Maintain Metering Equipment

Ensure water meter is functional and accurately recording abstraction.

5-Year Permit Renewal Process:

Permits expire after 5 years. Renewal process is simpler than initial application:

  • Submit Renewal Application: 3 months before expiry (WRA sends reminder)
  • Renewal Fee: KSH 3,000 processing + annual tariff
  • Site Re-Inspection: Only if significant changes occurred (new pump, increased usage)
  • Processing Time: 2-4 weeks (faster than initial application)

‚ö†Ô∏è Permit Suspension/Revocation Triggers

WRA can suspend or revoke your permit for:

  • Non-payment of annual fees for 2+ consecutive years
  • Exceeding permitted abstraction volumes
  • Failure to install/maintain metering equipment
  • Water pollution caused by improper borehole management
  • Selling water commercially without proper permit category
  • Obstructing WRA inspections or failing to provide records

Problem: Missing drilling reports, poor water quality data, or vague site maps lead to application rejection.
