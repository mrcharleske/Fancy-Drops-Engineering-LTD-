What is a Borehole? üíß

A borehole is a narrow shaft drilled into the ground to access underground water sources known as aquifers. Unlike shallow wells, boreholes are typically drilled to depths of 30-300 meters, providing access to cleaner, more reliable water sources that are less affected by seasonal variations and surface contamination.

In Kenya, boreholes have become increasingly important for:

  • Residential properties - Reliable water supply for homes and compounds
  • Agricultural operations - Irrigation for crops and livestock watering
  • Commercial buildings - Hotels, schools, hospitals, and manufacturing facilities
  • Community projects - Shared water sources for villages and settlements

The Borehole Drilling Process üèóÔ∏è

Understanding the drilling process helps you know what to expect and ensures quality control. Here's our step-by-step approach at Fancy Drops Engineering:

Step 1: Hydrogeological Survey

Before any drilling begins, we conduct a professional hydrogeological survey using:

  • Geophysical Equipment - Resistivity meters and electromagnetic conductivity tools
  • Geological Mapping - Analysis of rock formations and water-bearing layers
  • Water Yield Estimation - Predicting water quantity and quality
  • Site Selection - Identifying the optimal drilling location

Why it matters: A proper survey increases success rates from 60% to over 95% and helps avoid costly dry boreholes. In West Pokot and surrounding areas, geological knowledge is crucial due to varied terrain.

Step 2: Permit Application

We handle all paperwork with the Water Resources Authority (WRA):

  • Borehole drilling permit application
  • Site assessment approval
  • Water abstraction permit (after successful drilling)
  • Compliance with Water Act 2016 regulations

Step 3: Drilling

Our drilling process involves:

  • Site Preparation - Clearing and leveling the drilling area
  • Rotary Drilling - Using specialized drilling rigs to penetrate rock layers
  • Depth Monitoring - Continuous assessment as we drill deeper
  • Water Strike Detection - Identifying water-bearing zones
  • Casing Installation - Inserting steel or PVC pipes to prevent collapse

Step 4: Development & Testing

After reaching the target depth:

  • Borehole Development - Cleaning and improving water flow
  • Pumping Tests - Determining sustainable yield rates
  • Water Quality Testing - Laboratory analysis of water samples
  • Yield Certification - Official documentation of borehole capacity

Step 5: Pump Installation

We install the appropriate pumping system:

  • Submersible Pumps - For deep boreholes (most common)
  • Solar Pumping Systems - Eco-friendly, cost-effective option
  • Pressure Systems - For consistent water pressure
  • Storage Tanks - Adequate water storage capacity

WRA Regulations & Permits üìú

Kenya's Water Act 2016 regulates all borehole activities. Here's what you need to know:

Required Permits:

  • Drilling Permit - Must be obtained BEFORE drilling begins (KSH 10,000-15,000)
  • Water Abstraction Permit - Required after successful drilling for water extraction
  • Discharge Permit - If you plan to dispose of excess water

Application Process:

  1. Submit application to WRA regional office
  2. Provide site location (GPS coordinates)
  3. Wait for site inspection by WRA officers
  4. Receive approval (usually 2-4 weeks)
  5. Proceed with drilling

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Legal Requirements:

  • Drilling without a permit is ILLEGAL and punishable by fines or imprisonment
  • All boreholes must be registered with WRA
  • Commercial water usage requires additional permits
  • Annual water abstraction fees apply (based on usage volume)
  • Professional drillers must be licensed by WRA

Good news: At Fancy Drops Engineering, we handle ALL permit applications and compliance requirements on your behalf. We're fully licensed and registered with WRA.

Borehole Maintenance Requirements üîß

Proper maintenance ensures your borehole serves you reliably for 20-30+ years:

Annual Maintenance (Every 12 Months):

  • Pump inspection and servicing
  • Water quality testing
  • Borehole camera inspection (if available)
  • Electrical system check
  • Casing integrity assessment

Monthly Checks (DIY):

  • Check water flow rates
  • Listen for unusual pump sounds
  • Inspect visible pipes for leaks
  • Clean storage tanks
  • Test water pressure

Signs Your Borehole Needs Professional Attention:

  • Decreasing water yield
  • Cloudy or dirty water
  • Unusual pump noises or vibrations
  • Frequent pump cycling
  • Increased electricity consumption
  • Water with strange taste or odor

Common Mistakes to Avoid ‚ùå

1. Skipping the Hydrogeological Survey

The Risk: Drilling in the wrong location leads to dry boreholes or low yields. We've seen clients waste KSH 500,000+ on failed boreholes due to poor site selection.

Our Advice: ALWAYS invest in a professional survey. It costs KSH 30,000-60,000 but saves you from potentially losing hundreds of thousands.

2. Choosing the Cheapest Contractor

The Risk: Unqualified drillers use substandard materials, skip proper casing, or provide inaccurate depth reports. You might save 20% upfront but face 200% more costs in repairs and re-drilling.

Our Advice: Verify contractor licenses, check references, and ensure they use quality materials. At Fancy Drops, we provide detailed contracts and guarantees.

3. Undersizing the Pump

The Risk: A pump that's too small won't meet your water demands. Too large, and you waste electricity and risk damaging the borehole.

Our Advice: Let professionals calculate your actual water needs based on usage patterns, household size, or business requirements.

4. Ignoring Water Quality

The Risk: Borehole water can contain high iron, salts, or harmful bacteria. Untreated water damages plumbing and poses health risks.

Our Advice: Always conduct laboratory testing and install appropriate treatment systems (filters, softeners, UV sterilization).

5. Neglecting Maintenance

The Risk: Pumps fail prematurely, boreholes silt up, and water quality deteriorates. Emergency repairs cost 3-5 times more than scheduled maintenance.

Our Advice: Sign up for annual maintenance contracts. Prevention is always cheaper than emergency fixes.
