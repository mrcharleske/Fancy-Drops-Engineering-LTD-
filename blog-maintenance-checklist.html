Monthly Maintenance Checklist (15 Minutes/Month)

MONTHLY

Every 4 Weeks: Quick Health Check

üîç Visual Inspections

‚úÖ
Check Wellhead Seal Integrity

Look for cracks in concrete apron, gaps where insects/rodents could enter. No standing water should pool around the wellhead.

Red Flag: Cracked seal or water pooling ‚Üí Call technician immediately (contamination risk!)

‚úÖ
Inspect Storage Tank Condition

Check for leaks, algae growth, or debris accumulation. Ensure tank lid is securely closed (prevents mosquito breeding).

Warning: Algae inside tank ‚Üí Schedule cleaning within 2 weeks

‚úÖ
Examine All Visible Pipes & Joints

Look for rust spots, loose fittings, or dripping connections. Check pressure gauge (should read 2.5-4.0 bar for domestic systems).

Red Flag: Pressure below 2.0 bar ‚Üí Possible pump issues or leak

‚úÖ
Listen for Unusual Pump Sounds

Normal: Smooth humming when pump runs. Abnormal: Grinding, squealing, clicking, or excessive vibration.

Red Flag: Any unusual noise ‚Üí Call Fancy Drops for pump inspection

‚úÖ
Test Water Quality (Simple Test)

Fill clear glass with tap water. Check for: cloudiness, unusual color, odor, or visible particles.

Warning: Any abnormality ‚Üí Send sample for lab testing

‚úÖ
Verify Electrical Components

Check control panel for loose wires, burnt smells, or tripped breakers. Ensure pump switch functions properly.

Red Flag: Burnt smell or frequently tripping breaker ‚Üí Electrical fault (fire risk!)

üí° Pro Tip: Create a Maintenance Log

Use a simple notebook or phone app to record:

  • Date of inspection
  • Pressure gauge reading
  • Any abnormalities observed
  • Actions taken

Why? This log helps technicians diagnose issues faster and proves your maintenance history for warranty claims.

üìä Monthly Tasks - Time & Cost

  • Time Required: 15 minutes (can be done while doing other chores)
  • Cost: KSH 0 (you do it yourself!)
  • Tools Needed: Flashlight, notebook, clear glass
  • Best Time: Same day each month (set phone reminder)

Quarterly Maintenance Tasks (Every 3 Months)

QUARTERLY

Every 12 Weeks: Deep Dive Inspection

üßπ Cleaning & Servicing

‚úÖ
Clean Storage Tank Thoroughly

Process:

  1. Turn off pump and drain tank completely
  2. Scrub interior walls with clean brush + disinfectant solution
  3. Rinse thoroughly with clean water (3x minimum)
  4. Refill and add water treatment tablets if needed

DIY Cost: KSH 500 (disinfectant + treatment tablets) | Professional Service: KSH 3,000

‚úÖ
Test & Clean Water Filters

If you have sediment filters or carbon filters, inspect and clean/replace according to manufacturer guidelines.

Filter lifespan: Sediment (3-6 months), Carbon (6-12 months), UV bulbs (12 months)

Replacement Cost: Sediment filter KSH 1,500 | Carbon filter KSH 3,500 | UV bulb KSH 5,000

‚úÖ
Lubricate Moving Parts

Apply appropriate lubricant to: gate valves, ball valves, pump motor bearings (if accessible), pressure switch hinges.

Cost: KSH 800 (lubricant spray + grease)

‚úÖ
Check & Tighten All Bolts/Connections

Over time, vibrations loosen connections. Inspect and tighten: pipe joints, electrical terminals, control panel screws, tank support brackets.

Tools needed: Adjustable wrench, screwdrivers set

‚úÖ
Test Pump Performance

Simple Performance Test:

  • Time how long it takes to fill a 20L bucket from tap
  • Compare to baseline (record this during initial setup!)
  • If filling time increased by 30%+ ‚Üí Pump efficiency declining

Warning: Significant performance drop ‚Üí Schedule professional pump inspection

‚úÖ
Inspect Electrical Wiring & Connections

Look for: frayed wires, loose terminals, corrosion on connections, proper grounding. Test circuit breaker functionality.

Red Flag: Any electrical damage ‚Üí Hire licensed electrician immediately

üìä Quarterly Tasks - Time & Cost

  • Time Required: 2-3 hours (DIY) | 1 hour (professional service)
  • DIY Cost: KSH 2,000-3,500 (materials only)
  • Professional Service Cost: KSH 8,000-12,000 (Fancy Drops rate)
  • Recommendation: DIY if confident, professional every other quarter

Annual Comprehensive Servicing (Once Per Year)

ANNUAL

Every 12 Months: Professional Deep Service

This is the most critical maintenance activity. We STRONGLY recommend hiring professionals (like Fancy Drops) for annual servicing‚Äîit's too complex and risky for DIY.

üîß Professional Technician Tasks

‚úÖ
Complete Pump Inspection & Servicing

What we do:

  • Pull pump from borehole (requires specialized winch equipment)
  • Inspect impeller for wear, corrosion, or damage
  • Check motor bearings and seals
  • Test electrical continuity and insulation resistance
  • Replace worn parts (seals, bearings, impeller if needed)
  • Clean pump housing and intake screen
  • Reinstall and test performance

Cost: KSH 15,000-25,000 (includes labor + minor parts)

‚úÖ
Comprehensive Water Quality Testing

Laboratory tests include:

  • Bacterial contamination (E.coli, coliforms)
  • Chemical analysis (pH, TDS, nitrates, fluoride, heavy metals)
  • Physical properties (turbidity, color, taste, odor)
  • Compliance with KEBS standards

Cost: KSH 5,000-8,000 (KEBS-certified lab)

‚úÖ
Borehole Camera Inspection (Every 5 Years)

Lowering a waterproof camera down the borehole to inspect:

  • Casing integrity (cracks, corrosion, joint failures)
  • Screen condition (blockages, biofilm buildup)
  • Water level stability
  • Sediment accumulation at bottom

Cost: KSH 12,000-18,000 (recommended every 5 years, not annually)

‚úÖ
Control System Calibration

Test and recalibrate: pressure switch settings, water level sensors, automatic pump controllers, electrical protection systems.

Cost: KSH 3,000-5,000

‚úÖ
Electrical Safety Audit

Licensed electrician checks: grounding system, RCD (residual current device) functionality, wiring insulation, protection against lightning/power surges.

Critical for safety! Prevents electrocution and fire hazards.

Cost: KSH 4,000-6,000

‚úÖ
Solar System Check (If Applicable)

For solar-powered pumps, inspect: panel efficiency, battery health, charge controller, inverter performance, cable connections.

Cost: KSH 5,000-8,000 (included in Fancy Drops solar maintenance packages)

Warning Signs That Demand Immediate Attention

üö® EMERGENCY: Call Technician Within 24 Hours

  • No Water Flow: Pump runs but no water comes out ‚Üí Possible pump failure or pipe burst
  • Muddy/Brown Water: Sudden water discoloration ‚Üí Casing breach or aquifer contamination
  • Burning Smell: From control panel or pump area ‚Üí Electrical fault (fire risk!)
  • Pump Won't Start: Despite electrical power being on ‚Üí Motor failure or controller issue
  • Constant Pump Cycling: Pump starts/stops every few seconds ‚Üí Pressure tank failure or leak
  • Sewage Smell in Water: Foul odor from taps ‚Üí Contamination emergency!
  • Significant Yield Drop: Water output reduced by 50%+ suddenly ‚Üí Aquifer depletion or pump damage

üí° Warning Signs of Gradual Issues (Schedule Service Within 2 Weeks)

  • Water pressure slowly declining over months
  • Increased electricity bills without usage change
  • Pump takes longer to fill tank than before
  • Occasional cloudy water (clears after running)
  • Minor leaks at pipe joints
  • Pump noise gradually getting louder
  • Water has slight taste/odor (not normal for your water)
